WHAT IS SAMYAK ?

Samyak is a non-profitable trust based in Jaipur.It was established on to pioneer its young founders’ missions to convert their aspiration: Altruism knows no boundaries, into reality. The trust aims to be a vehicle of dissemination, dissemination of self subsistence over blind dependence. With this goal in mind, Samyak has collaborated with several long forgotten and inaccessible villages and virtuous NGOs in Rajasthan which are deprived of aid from the external community. The scorching sun has burnt the villagers’ ambitions and livelihood and thus they revel in the underbelly of inaccessibility. They haven’t seen the world as we have, they aren’t aware of the opportunities as we are, and solely due to that they remain hopeless. Samyak envisions a day when India thrives in parity and prosperity. Samyak’s initial goal is to collect dry food, clothes, blankets, books, toys, materials that these underprivileged Indians are deprived of to let them know that we see them and we care. Then we aim to expand as a bridge between the rural community and the urban by developing their skills, funding their talent and showcasing their produce/handicrafts in the cities. Our vision is to see a world where maximum opportunities and resources are being sustainably utilized and that no area, no livelihood, no happiness is lost into oblivion.

THE TEAM

Priyal Gupta

Co-Founder

However, when juxtaposed with the stereotypical generation of seventeen year olds today whose ambition wraps itself around the corporate, business mogul themed life, Priyal’s ambitions may sound atypical. She strongly feels that “Opportunity is a door that many are deprived of” and to answer that question has established Samyak, with her co-founder Shivani, in the hopes of being the architect of that door for those without it. She exudes a pro-active aura while she works and her cage of determination seems indestructible. She’s not easily stirred once committed and is a maverick planner be it planning her little cousin’s princess party or awareness drives. When she’s not engaged in her altruistic pursuits, you might find her engrossed deeply in her Sitar. She loves solving social issues plaguing her motherland as much as she does Math problems. If you ever find yourself looking into her eyes, you’ll notice an ebullient spark- and that’ll tell you all you need to know about her.

Shivani Jain

Co-Founder

Shivani, at the age of seventeen, beliefs in two spheres of life: learning and applying. She is a fervent advocate of the faction which believes that ones knowledge goes to waste until it helps humanity progress forward. Just her body language is enough to assure you of her leadership capabilities and once you’ve talked to her, you’ll never forget the impact she would’ve left on you.





THE NOBLE THINGS WE DO

FOOD FOR ALL

Samyak envisions a world where ‘hungry stomachs’ is a Loch Ness monster i.e. it doesn’t exist. One of the main causes of social degradation is food deprivation which results in no physical energy to work and thus extinguishes even the littlest of hopes humans hold-to work, to live, to succeed. After visiting several orphanages and far off villages, Samyak decided to vanquish hunger- at least as much as is within its ability. Doesn’t it make you cringe whenever you see an unfortunate kid wailing because of hunger? So do we and so we propose this: Together we can fight hunger, together we can uplift lost souls. Join us in our mission.

SUBSISTENCE NOT DEPENDENCE

Often as humans, especially in India, we depend on our parents for a lot of things- from cooking our dinners to packing our suitcases. This situation is much aggravated especially when the unprivileged get over dependent on their donors. That makes them lethargic and laidback thus, they become uninterested in doing anything to sustain themselves because they rely so heavily on their ‘fall back ons’. Samyak, in the long term, wants to propagate its efforts towards helping the rural community achieve employment and better earnings through microfinance schemes, skill development, craft making, etc. Because Samyak believes in Ayn Rand’s philosophy deeply: A man’s core justification for his existence is his ability, it asks all its supporters to join its endeavours.

DISASTER RELIEF & REHAB.

Samyak is aware that its charity is not restricted by boundaries for kindness knows no limit therefore, it has taken an oath to raise awareness if, god forbid, any natural calamity disturbs the global community. Samyak has, early in its onset, conducted and provided earthquake relief support to Nepal and plans to do so in future- be it any nation. Samyak believes that international aid is of as much paramount importance as national or local; therefore it joins hands with the world citizens to fight destruction- of love, mankind, and hope.

SAMYAK IN ACTION

Visit to alienated villages in Aamli

May 4th: Samyak visited the circlet of alienated villages in Aamli, Sawai Madhopur. We donated to them some bananas, biscuits, etc and talked with them about their condition vis a vis.

Nepal Earthquake Relief donation drives

May 15th- 16th: Samyak conducted Nepal Earthquake Relief donation drives on May 15th to 16th in collaboration with Goonj. The team knocked on doors in approximately 6 neighborhoods in Jaipur to collect donations for the Nepal Relief and spread awareness about Samyak's objectives.

Second Nepal Earthquake Relief Drive

Samyak collected donations first hand through awareness drives and volunteer contribution among numerous localities within Jaipur for the Nepal Earthquake Relief.



Donations to Naya Sawera

On June 6th,Samyak received generous donations of myriad of nutritious dry food from its donors in Jaipur this week.We felt that this 25 kg donation would be best channelled through local donations and so we personally visited Naya Sawera- an NGO that specializes in catering to the quotidian lives of HIV positive kids.

Joint effort donation drive with VHO

June 9th: Samyak put in joint efforts with VHP and donated 45 kgs of Dry ration obtained from its generous donors. VHP is an organisation prevalent in over 80 countries in the world and it works towards expanding faith in religion and humanity. In doing so, it acts as a sanctuary for the members of the Indian community and supports them physically, mentally and emotionally

Food Ration donation to Missionary of Charity Trust of Mother Teresa Home

July 4: Today, Samyak went to Missionary of Charity Trust of Mother Teresa Home to donate some food ration. This trust facilitates from shelter to food to rehabilitation for those who've been neglected and left alone like aged people or those strayed on roads or railway stations. Although Samyak didn't get to interact with the inhabitants because it was their sleeping time in the afternoon but it talked to the amicable sisters about how they can help.
